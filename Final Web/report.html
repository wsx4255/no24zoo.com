"I MADE IT!"

Rona Tz-Yu Chen, Year1 Student, Univerisity of London

As a beginner in the web development field, I was anxious if I could produce a completed work including html, CSS and javascript, especially it was required to be with responsive web design. It was a long journey and I finally made one that may not so perfect, but I like it a lot, after so much struggling and hard after-work nights.

For the whole framework, I based on the open source “bootstrap” to develop the basic responsive structure. In addition, I integrated several excellent free licensed samples from GitHub and Google search to improve my website design.

This website is aimed to become a general guide for first-time visitors to Taiwan. Being a Taiwanese, I love my country. Even if we are a small island, we have wonderful views, foods, people, culture, and technology due to our special geographic position. Thus, I would like to share how fabulous Taiwan is to the world.

However, due to the tight schedule and limited ability, I focused more on how to build up the website and codes instead of the content. I hope you can enjoy this online magazine with the beautiful pictures, and explore the spots yourselves after COVID-19. Welcome to Taiwan and if you have any traveling questions, don’t hesitate to contact me via the email: wsx4255@gmail.com. Thanks!

The Reflection

A. Three Things

Inspiration
  1. Youtuber CSScoke:

    I really appreciate his training video when I was struggling with how to start the first step of bootstrap. His detailed explanation helps me to build up the first page without too much frustration, and build a clear knowledge how to control the containers in different sizes. After watching his video, I finally had an idea that what would my website look like.

  2. Tourism Bureau of Taiwan:

    Thanks to the plentiful material provided on the official website of Tourism Bureau of Taiwan, which allows me to focus more on my codes instead of spending a lot of time taking relevant pictures from the huge internet world. The information they provided is clear and helped me to come up with the original structure.

  3. Some of my working partners in Germany:

    My colleagues who have never been to Asia have a lot of curiosity about Taiwan, and always asked me to share the beautiful point views here. Thus, I believe they are the best of the first audiences. Hope this “online show book” can give them more imagination of Taiwan!

Accessibility
  1. Images with "alt" tags:

    To make sure the images are defined with semantic words for the visually impaired people.

  2. Avoid using red & green color in the same area:

    To differentiate these two colors are hard for people who have red-green color blindness.

  3. Add tag”role” or ”aria-label” at the button:

    To make sure the accessibility tree can read the actions.

Usability
  1. Consistency of the main structure:

    The layout of the navigation and cover photos are the same in each page to make sure the end users know where they can find another pages.

  2. Visibility of the click action:

    If the end users click on the button, the color will be changed or showed different UI design to make the user behavior more straight-forward.

  3. Aesthetic design:

    Get rid of any unrelated decoration in every page to make sure the end users will only focus on the targeted information.

Things Learned
  1. How to build up the bootstrap:

    As a beginner, I was used to code HTML and CSS one line by one line directly, and this is the first time I referred to the open sources and increase much efficiency of the work. It cost several days to be familiar with the structure and application logics, but after I realized how to use it, it helps me upgrade not only the quality of the website but also the working time.

  2. How to integrate other good samples in my website:

    “Something look great may not fit in your world.” That was my lesson learned after several trials to copy others’ codes in my website. At the beginning, when I saw a beautiful design, I just copied and pasted in my page. However, that didn’t work well as everyone has their own logic in their codes in html, CSS, and js. If I would like to have the format 100% like them, I need to copy their whole CSS or framework to get the same idea. Therefore, I spent much time figuring out where is the most suitable part I can use and changing the tags or formats with my CSS codes. To be honest, integration sometimes is harder than producing things myself from 0 to 1, but when I saw the pretty and clean framework, I believe it deserves the effort.

  3. How to carefully produce every tiny part:

    During the whole coding process, I felt like a baby to learn the new language. For example, when I would like to indicate “km2”, I even don’t know how to make the “2” smaller and go up to the right position. Thanks for Google, it makes everyone has a super fast way to grab the knowledge what they don’t know. I also checked a lot of blogs sharing from experts to adjust my codes and make the specific functions happen.

B. Self Assessment

Making a website may be an easy work to professionals, but for me, a freshman just stepped into the coding world, it required a plenty of effort to overcome the frustration and challenges.

First, I would like to thanks myself for insisting on the course and the finishing large web project. It is easy for a business person to find a busy excuse to give up things outside the job. No matter how good or bad is this website, at least I’ve followed the games rules and the deadline.

Second, for the part I am proud of is the consistent and comfortable visual design. My code is simple and not so many skills, but I hope the audiences can enjoy the content and the layout. Also, I adjusted several details to make the pages look much cleaner and clearer. For example, instead of making every picture big enough to show, I learned how to do the "gallery" by javascript.

On the other hand, I believe there is still a long learning distance for me from becoming a web expert. For example, maybe some specific functions can be conducted by few codes, but I wrote a lot to make it work. In addition, I found the javascript is an endless amazing tool that I should spend more time exploring it and insert those animations to make the website cooler and more user-friendly.

To sum up, as the first personal project, I believe it worths a nice score in my mind, but to be more excellent, I should work hard and keep learning to make the information become my own knowledge!
